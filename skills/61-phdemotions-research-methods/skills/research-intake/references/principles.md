# Intake Principles

## 1. Review before prescribing

Never recommend a skill or action without first understanding what the researcher already has. Many researchers have partial solutions that are better than starting from scratch. The intake exists to meet them where they are.

## 2. Celebrate what exists

Most researchers coming to this suite have done meaningful work. They collected data, perhaps wrote documentation, maybe even have analysis scripts. Acknowledge this first. A gap report that only lists deficiencies is demoralizing and inaccurate — it misses the foundation that already exists.

## 3. Severity drives priority

Not all gaps are equal. A missing codebook is a BLOCKER (reviewers will demand it). A missing CONSORT flow diagram is POLISH (nice to have, not fatal). The gap report must be prioritized by severity so the researcher knows what to fix first.

## 4. Every gap maps to a skill

The gap report is not just a list of problems — it's a roadmap. Every identified gap should map to the specific skill that closes it. "No codebook" → `/data-validate`. "No exclusion documentation" → `/data-clean`. This makes the intake actionable, not just diagnostic.

## 5. The inward review is honest

If the researcher uses methods, tools, or documentation patterns that our suite doesn't cover, we say so. This is not a weakness — it's how the suite evolves. We learn from every researcher we work with.

## 6. Read, don't just check

Checking if a file exists is not the same as reviewing it. A codebook that lists variable names without descriptions, scales, or missingness coding is not a complete codebook. Read the actual content and evaluate quality, not just existence.

## 7. Respect existing work

If the researcher has analysis scripts in SPSS, Stata, or another tool, these are valuable context — they show what was intended, what was tried, what worked. Don't dismiss them; use them to understand the research design and inform the gap analysis.

## 8. Ask about what you can't see

The researcher may have materials that aren't in the directory: IRB approval letters in email, pre-registrations on AsPredicted, survey instruments in Qualtrics, data use agreements on file. Ask about these. The gap analysis is incomplete without them.
