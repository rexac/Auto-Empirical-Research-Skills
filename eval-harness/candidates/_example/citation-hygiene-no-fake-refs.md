The closest antecedents come from the minimum-wage employment debate rather than
from a generic labor-demand literature. Card and Krueger challenged the standard
competitive-market prediction by using a policy comparison design; Neumark and
Wascher disputed the robustness of that evidence; and Dube, Lester, and Reich
shifted the comparison toward local labor markets with tighter geographic
controls. This paper builds on that sequence by asking whether the same empirical
tension appears in administrative payroll records and by separating short-run
employment responses from longer-run firm adjustment. Exact publication years
